Margarita Geleta

TL;DR: I am a CS PhD student at UC Berkeley. My main research interest is generative modelling.


I worked at Amazon as an Applied Scientist in HIT, and at Stanford as a Research Assistant in the Biomedical Data Science department.


I did my undergrad at UPC BarcelonaTech and researched in the Image UPC group. Before transferring to Berkeley, I started grad school at UC Irvine where I worked in the ETAD lab and collaborated with the UCI NLP group.

Maestro: Multi-level Attack and Defense Simulation Environment for AI Education and Research

Margarita Geleta, Jiacen Xu, Manikanta Loya, Junlin Wang, Sameer Singh, Zhou Li and Sergio Gago Masague   [Under Review]

Maintainer of the Maestro leaderboard and tester of the framework, providing a service for cybersecurity educational purposes in the domain of adversarial attacks and defenses.

  • Leaderboard repository at UCI NLP Group

PixInWav: Residual Steganography for Hiding Pixels in Audio

Margarita Geleta, Cristina Punti, Kevin McGuinness, Jordi Pons, Cristian Canton, Xavier Giro-i-Nieto   [ICASSP 2022] & [WiCV/CVPR 2021]

A pioneering work on hidding images within audio waveforms, showing real results retrieving images from recorded audio waves. Steganography is called “the art of hiding” - it arranges the methods that are capable of hiding information at plain sight.

  • Full-length paper Arxiv preprint GitHub repository

Generative Moment Matching Networks for Genotype Simulation

Maria Perera, Daniel Mas Montserrat, Míriam Barrabés, Margarita Geleta, Xavier Giró-i-Nieto, Alexander G. Ioannidis  [EMBC 2022]

We explore the use of Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMNs) for SNP simulation and also as a useful tool for privacy-preserving data sharing mechanism for modern day biobanks, and most importantly, a way to partially mitigate population bias within datasets.

  • Full-length paper at bioArxiv

Deep Variational Autoencoders for Population Genetics

Margarita Geleta, Daniel Mas Montserrat, Carlos D. Bustamante, Xavier Giró-i-Nieto, Alexander G. Ioannidis  [ASHG 2021] & [DLBCN 2021]

An exhaustive study of Variational Autoencoders in the context of dimensionality reduction, classification, compression, imputation and simulation of genomic data with interesting discussions about human populations.
