Rita Geleta's website

The ubiquity of data and the access to vast computational power has triggered a tectonic shift that is incrementally changing the way we communicate, work, interact, and more generally, live. What decades ago was just an unattainable quixotic dream, today it is becoming a reality - we have systems and tools that can analyze, take actions and reduce the uncertainty of the near future.

Looking with hindsight, the achievements of connectionist systems are impressive but many times they lack understanding. Deep Learning is built on many heuristics, which reliably predict outcomes of experiments, yet oftentimes they are not defined mathematically and the lack of some rigorous or pellucid definitions can lead to the vision of Deep Neural Networks as “black boxes” and, furthermore, research starts being a chase behind state-of-the-art architectures and a phenomenon called “stacking more layers” is taking place, without a real understanding of the decision rules which could lead to the gist of the improvement.


In my view, the goal of any scientific field is to build understanding, interpretability & intuition behind perhaps complex, yet naturally motivated, algorithms and techniques. It may not seem so crucial, although I have noticed that this, in relatively short-term, can become the bottleneck of research.
