Audio Steganography

Steganography (from Greek, "steganos" - concealed or covered, "graphein" - writing) is the method of concealing a secret signal within a cover signal. Those embedded signals may be sent through public channels and the idea behind steganography is to hide them in a way that the intended secret signal does not attract attention to itself, so that the unintended recipients stay unaware of the secret communication.

I was very interested in audio steganography (hiding stuff in audio) and learned lots of surprising things in this field. Go ahead and read about it.

Hate Speech

There was a time when I was really interested in learning what were the features that differentiated hate speech and toxic words from unoffensive speech / words. I conducted a series of works and made pretty cool visualizations: I reimplemented the String Subsequence Kernel (SSK-SVM) for text classiification and trained BERT-based deep learning models for sentiment analysis.

Trivia: because I provided the only one implementation of SSK-SVM in R, GitHub awarded me with the Arctic Code Vault Contributor in 2020!
